I love coding - I get so much satisfaction from coming up with working solutions, solving real problems and automating tasks so that my time may be better spent elsewhere.

Another reason I am so drawn to coding is because it and tech intersect with and are required in nearly every other field, including my other main passion: animals!

I grew up in a homeschooling community in the Texas wilderness where I developed a very deep love and appreciation for animals. Much of my motivation comes from the desire to help protect them in whatever way I can.

That's why, in 5th grade when I realized how far I had fell behind my peers academically, I asked my mother if we could move to Austin so that I could attend a public school. Thankfully, she agreed.

My most proud accomplishment is going from receiving the exact math standardized test score I needed to advance into 6th grade and being placed in remedial classes to graduating at the top of my class in high school and receiving the highest score possible in the AP calculus exam. The transition and reframing the way I thought about learning and approached school was extremely grueling at times.

However, that process allowed me to hone skills that benefit me every day:

  1. I am extremely detail-oriented.

  2. I have grit.

  3. I am a self-starter and quick learner.

In my spare time I enjoy drawing, creating and modifying clothing or thrifted items, song-writing, working on personal projects, video games, and hanging out with my cat.
